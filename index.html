Data Sharing Decision Helper

Step 1 — What are you planning to share?
Option A — Low-detail content

Examples:

  • Selfie at your poster
  • Glossy/blurred poster where text is unreadable
  • Room/venue photos, general vibes
Low risk

Guidance:

  • Safe to share on social media.
  • Optional courtesy: tell PI if poster is in background.
  • Avoid zooming in to make data readable.
Option B — High-detail content

Examples:

  • Clean PDF of poster
  • Readable data/figures
  • Slides, code, notebooks
  • Datasets or volumes

→ Continue to Step 2 below.

Step 2 — Is there any written rule or policy about this?
2A — Yes, there is a written rule.

Go to Step 3.

2B — No rule / Not sure / Vague.

Treat as unclear → Go to Step 4 (ownership).

Step 3 — If a rule exists, does it clearly allow this?
3A — Yes, the rule clearly allows this.
Low risk

Guidance:

  • Share is allowed (explicit policy).
  • Acknowledge collaborators / facilities.
  • Save a copy/screenshot of the rule.
3B — No / ambiguous / full of wiggle words.

Treat as unclear → Go to Step 4.

Step 4 — Who owns / controls this content?
4A — Only you (no core data, no collaborators, no human/industry data).
Medium risk

Guidance:

  • Send one short timestamped message:
  • “I plan to upload X to Y on <date>. Any objections?”
  • If no objection, and no policy says otherwise → safe to proceed.
  • Keep that message as proof of good faith.
4B — Shared control (PI, collaborators, core facility, funder, human/clinical).
High risk

Guidance:

  • Do not share without explicit written approval.
  • If approval denied → don't post.
  • If needed for portfolio → consider redacted/synthetic versions.

This is a decision aid, not legal advice. Medium/high risk → always document that you asked, and wait for clear approval.
